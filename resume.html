Historical Overview

1917
Birth

Ferdinand Marcos Sr. was born in Sarrat, Ilocos Norte, Philippines.

Ferdinand Marcos was born on September 11, 1917, in Sarrat, a town in the province of Ilocos Norte, Philippines. He was the second child of Mariano Marcos, a lawyer and politician, and Josefa Edralin, a schoolteacher.

1965
Presidency

Elected President

In 1965, Ferdinand Marcos Sr. was elected President of the Philippines, becoming the tenth president of the country. His presidency initially showed promise with infrastructure projects and economic development.

1972
Martial Law

Declaration of Martial Law

In 1972, amidst rising political unrest and insurgency, Ferdinand Marcos Sr. declared Martial Law, citing the need to suppress communist and Muslim rebel threats. This move allowed him to rule by decree, extending his tenure in power.

1986
Fall from Power

People Power Revolution

The People Power Revolution in 1986 marked the culmination of years of opposition to Marcos' authoritarian rule. Mass protests, supported by key military and political figures, led to his ouster and exile to Hawaii.

College Education
University of the Philippines

Law School Years

Marcos studied law at the University of the Philippines (UP) in Manila, attending the College of Law. He excelled in both curricular and extra-curricular activities, becoming a member of the university's swimming, boxing, and wrestling teams. He was also an accomplished orator, debater, and writer for the student newspaper. While attending the UP College of Law, he became a member of the Upsilon Sigma Phi, where he met his future colleagues in government and some of his staunchest critics.

When he sat for the 1939 Bar Examinations, he was a bar topnotcher (top scorer) with a score of 92.35%. He graduated cum laude and was in the top ten of his class, with future Chief Justice Felix Makasiar becoming their class salutatorian. He was elected to the Pi Gamma Mu and the Phi Kappa Phi international honor societies, the latter giving him its Most Distinguished Member Award 37 years later.

Ferdinand Marcos received an honorary Doctor of Laws (LL.D.) (honoris causa) degree in 1967 from Central Philippine University. This recognition underscores his contributions and achievements in the field of law and public service.
